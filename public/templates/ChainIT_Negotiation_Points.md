## Critical Issues Requiring Resolution

### 1. Commission Holdback Structure (Section 4.2)

**Current Terms:**
- 25% annual holdback with year-end release
- Subject to "performance review" before release

**Market Standard:**
- 10-15% holdback with quarterly releases
- Objective release criteria (not subjective review)

**Business Impact:**
- Current terms create severe cash flow constraints that prevent reinvestment in sales infrastructure
- Example: $100K earned = $25K held for 12+ months

**Proposed Revision:**
- **15% holdback with quarterly releases** (Q1-Q4)
- Release contingent on objective metrics: customer retention >85%, no fraud/breach incidents
- First release after 90 days (not 12 months)

---

### 2. Commission Rate Structure & Longevity (Section 3.1)

**Current Terms:**
- Year 1: 12% | Year 2: 8% | Year 3: 4% | Year 4+: 0%
- Commissions cease after 36 months despite ongoing customer value

**Market Standard:**
- 20-35% commission rates
- Perpetual or minimum 5-year commission duration
- Flat rate or modest step-downs (not 67% reduction)

**Business Impact:**
- Financial modeling shows current rates require $18M-$21M annual sales pipeline to achieve $150K net income
- 3-year cliff creates perverse incentive to churn customers before Year 4
- Cannot justify long-term customer success investment when commissions evaporate

**Proposed Revision:**
- **Year 1: 20% | Year 2: 15% | Year 3+: 10% (perpetual)**
- Alternatively: Flat 15% with no step-downs or cliffs
- Minimum 5-year commission guarantee per customer contract

---

### 3. Undefined "Baseline Usage" (Section 3.1.a)

**Current Issue:**
- Contract references "baseline usage" as threshold for commission eligibility
- No definition provided for what constitutes "baseline"
- Creates ambiguity and commission dispute risk

**Business Impact:**

- Baselines fixed at contract signing (not retroactively adjustable)
- Example clarity: "Baseline usage = $10K annual for Tier 1 customers"

### 4. Processing Commission Calculation (Section 3.2.b)

**Current Terms:**
- 5-10% commission on "net processing margin" (not gross volume)
- No written formula for margin calculation
- Reality: $1M processing volume = ~$3,060 margin = $153-$306 commission

**Market Standard:**
- Transparent margin calculations with written formulas
- Higher commission rates (10-15%) to offset low margin base

**Business Impact:**
- Processing commissions essentially negligible despite requiring significant customer volume
- Cannot effectively sell processing without understanding economics

**Proposed Revision:**
- **Written formula for margin calculation included in contract**
- **10% commission on net margin** (eliminate 5% tier)
- Margin floor guarantee: Minimum $500 margin per $1M processing volume

---

### 5. Chargeback Liability (Pactvera MSA Section 7.3)

**Current Terms:**
- ISP liable for customer chargebacks despite no control over service delivery
- No cap on chargeback deductions from commissions

**Business Impact:**
- Unlimited downside risk from factors completely outside ISP control
- Could erase months of commission earnings from single customer incident
- Industry average: 0.5-2% chargeback rate

**Proposed Revision:**
- **Chargeback liability capped at 0.5% of ISP's total earned commissions**
- ISP only liable for chargebacks directly caused by ISP misrepresentation or fraud
- Written notification within 30 days of chargeback with dispute rights

---

### 6. Payment Terms & Frequency

**Current Terms:**
- Payment timing undefined beyond "after activation and settlement"
- Appears to be monthly payment cycles with annual holdback release

**Market Standard:**
- Quarterly payment cycles for main commissions
- Monthly commission statements for transparency

**Proposed Revision:**
- **Quarterly payment cycles** (Net 30 after quarter close)
- **Monthly commission statements** detailing: gross earned, holdback, deductions, net payable
- Payment within 30 days of customer invoice payment (not indefinite delay)

---

### 7. 1099 Contractor Status Without Base Compensation

**Current Terms:**
- Pure commission, zero base salary
- 1099 status = 42.3% total tax burden (self-employment + federal + state)

**Market Consideration:**
- Most ISP programs offer base salary ($60K-$80K) + commission OR
- Dramatically higher commission rates to offset 1099 risk

**Business Impact:**
- Zero income stability during ramp period (6-12 months)
- Cannot sustain operations without capital reserves

**Proposed Revision:**
- **Option A:** $60K annual base salary + current commission structure
- **Option B:** Increase commission rates per Item #2 above + remain 1099
- **Option C:** Quarterly minimum commission guarantee ($15K/quarter) during Year 1 ramp period
